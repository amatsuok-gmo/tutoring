About

Tutoring built on
the assertion that French
is a global language.

I'm Aaron Matsuoka — a recent graduate with a B.A in Organizational Studies with Honors and a B.A. in French & Francophone Studies from Pitzer and Scripps Colleges and a JBHS alumni. After growing up in Redding, I've lived and worked in Strasbourg, France and Geneva, Switzerland. My approach recognizes that French is a global language, spoken beyond the borders of Continental Europe. I treat language as culture: we read, discuss, and think in French from day one. I hope to expose my students to the whole Francophone world: including but not limited to, Francophone Africa, Canada, Polynesia, Historical Indochina, and the Caribbean. I aim to help my student develop a real working proficiency of the language, not just skills that are only relevant the classroom.

Services

What we work on together.

Sessions are tailored to your level and goals. Whether you're preparing for an exam or simply want to hold a conversation, there's a path for you.

01
Conversational French

Build fluency through guided discussion on topics that interest you — film, food, current events, literature. No textbook required.

02
Academic writing

Essay structure, argumentation, and style in French. Ideal for university students and those preparing for DELF examinations.

03
Grammar & pronunciation

Targeted work on the mechanics that hold you back — verb moods, liaison, register — with clear explanations grounded in real usage. For those who are interested I have working knowledge of IPA, a useful tool for understanding phonetics in any language.

04
Cultural Artifacts

Read Albert Camus, Aimé Cesaire or Annie Ernaux with someone who studied them seriously. Watch films from Angès Varda to Alice Diop. Perfect for learners who want French to feel more like a muscle than a skill.
